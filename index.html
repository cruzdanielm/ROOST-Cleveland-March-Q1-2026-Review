Executive Summary

March delivered a breakout month — Q1 inflected sharply, closing the seasonal gap to plan by $52K.

March delivered $266K of Rooms Revenue, 22.8% ahead of budget and 30.3% above prior year, at 67.9% occupancy against a 62.5% plan and $201 ADR against a $178 plan — rate and volume both outperformed. Gross Operating Profit of $80K came in $53K ahead of budget (+193%) and EBITDA of $55K beat plan by $53K and swung +$70K year over year. Quarter-to-date, the asset delivered $72K of GOP and a ($7)K EBITDA result — $57K (+370%) ahead of plan on GOP and $52K better than the budgeted Q1 EBITDA loss of ($59)K. The story is clear: March's inflection point broke Cleveland out of the seasonal trough and established a materially improved trajectory heading into peak season.

The one-line read

March was the inflection — rate and volume moved together, and the dollars showed up on the bottom line. ADR lifted $23 above budget (+12.9%) and $30 above prior year (+17.5%), while occupancy ran 5.4 points ahead of plan. Q1 as a whole still finished in a seasonal EBITDA loss, but closed the budgeted gap by $52K — roughly 90% of which came from March. The watch items heading into Q2 are (1) sustaining March's rate discipline across April and May shoulder dates, (2) managing the growing concentration in the Cleveland Charge account (now 21.9% of Q1 Rooms Revenue), and (3) protecting the Q1 YoY improvement as Property Operations, Insurance, and Utilities expense pressures emerged against budget.

What drove the quarter

Performance highlights

  • March breakout. Rooms Revenue +$49K vs budget / +$62K vs PY; EBITDA of $55K vs $2K plan — a $53K monthly beat on an unchanged Undistributed cost base.
  • ADR discipline. March ADR $200.88 vs $178.00 budget (+$22.88, +12.9%) and +$29.93 vs PY (+17.5%). Q1 ADR $167.01 vs $156.00 plan (+$11.01, +7.1%) and +$15.95 vs PY (+10.6%).
  • Rooms expense efficiency. Q1 Rooms Expenses $140K vs $189K budget — $49K under plan on housekeeping labor, laundry, linen, and operating supplies as the labor model scaled the extended-stay mix.
  • Cleveland Charge anchor. #1 account at $124K YTD (+$25K / +25% YoY), 1,047 room nights — the single largest contract block in the book.

Strategic areas of focus

Three items to manage into Q2

Rate sustainability. March showed Cleveland can price rate; Q1 YTD ADR still trails the comp set by $7.66 (ARI 95.61). Holding the March rate profile through April and May shoulder nights is the most valuable single lever.

Account concentration. Cleveland Charge represents ~22% of Q1 Rooms Revenue at a $118 ADR — critical volume but rate-dilutive. National Corporate Housing ($32K new) and the broader corporate-housing tier are the natural diversifiers.

Variable cost pressure. Q1 Property Operations +$20K over budget, Insurance +$7K, and Utilities +$7K offset roughly half the rooms-expense savings. Reining these back to plan unlocks the next layer of EBITDA.

What moved the month

Three drivers behind the $53K EBITDA beat vs budget

1 · Rate outperformed plan. ADR of $201 ran $23 ahead of budget (+12.9%). Event demand (Stereophonic Broadway run), Cleveland Charge home stands, and healthier OTA flex pricing all contributed. March delivered the first month of clear rate expansion above budget since property opening.

Revenue & Expense Drivers

What actually moved March & Q1
Demand · Rate · Mix · Cost
Drivers · March & Q1 YTD

Rate did the work on revenue; Rooms labor and I&T did the work on cost.

March's Rooms Revenue beat came from rate and volume together — ADR +$23 vs plan and +8.6% on rooms sold — while Q1 as a whole leaned on rate (+$11 ADR vs plan) against a modestly softer room-night base. On the cost side, Rooms Expenses ran $49K under plan YTD on housekeeping labor and operating-supply leverage, partially offset by pressure in Property Operations (+$20K), Utilities (+$7K), and Insurance (+$7K) — the three variance lines to reset in Q2.

Revenue drivers

Where the top-line beat originated

  • March rate expansion. ADR $200.88 vs $178.00 plan (+12.9%) and +$29.93 vs PY (+17.5%). Stereophonic Broadway run, Cleveland Charge home stands, and improved OTA flex pricing.
  • Q1 ADR premium over plan. ADR $167.01 vs $156.00 budget (+$11 / +7.1%) — rate lifted even as volume moderated, the highest-value composition change of the quarter.
  • Cleveland Charge anchor. $124K consolidated, +$25K / +25% YoY across 1,047 RN. The single largest production block in the book.
  • Corporate-housing ramp. National Corporate Housing $32K new, Weichert $4K new, Grant Thornton $11K new — fresh long-stay demand absorbed the step-down from legacy CRS, Nomad, and Corporate Living partners.
  • Rocket/Bedrock entry. Rocket Central $10K new (38 RN at $260 ADR) — first Q1 production of the Rocket ecosystem in Cleveland.
  • Direct-channel reactivation. ROOST Cleveland Direct Sales $14K vs $0.7K PY — Direct channel producing meaningfully again.

Expense drivers

What held the cost base below plan — and what pushed above

  • Rooms labor efficiency. Rooms S&W $63K YTD vs $82K budget (-$19K, -23%). Housekeeping model flexed against the long-stay mix.
  • Other Rooms Expenses. $44K YTD vs $73K budget (-$29K, -39%). Laundry, linen, operating supplies, and group commissions all under plan.
  • Information & Telecom. $32K vs $41K budget (-$8K, -21%). Tech-stack spend running efficient.
  • Administrative & G&A. $106K vs $114K (-$8K, -7%).
  • Sales & Marketing. $108K vs $113K (-$5K, -4%). Spend held roughly at plan; lower-than-planned commissions offset incremental media.
  • Property Operations & Maintenance. $68K vs $48K (+$20K, +40%). Elevated preventive-maintenance and repair activity — the single largest budget over-run YTD.
  • Utilities. $45K vs $39K (+$7K, +17%). Water/sewer timing; electricity and gas in line with plan.
  • Insurance. $31K vs $24K (+$7K, +29%). Premium step-up on renewal.
Bottom line

The revenue algorithm changed — rate did the work, and it sustained. The Q1 story is not a one-time March win: ADR ran $11 above plan for the full quarter, which is a durable mix/yield signal. On cost, $49K of rooms-expense savings dropped cleanly to Departmental Profit; but roughly $34K of that Benefit was consumed by Property Operations, Utilities, and Insurance pressures before reaching EBITDA. Resetting the three over-budget Undistributed categories — or absorbing them with April–June rate and volume — is the clearest path to closing the remaining EBITDA gap to plan in Q2.

Risks & Opportunities · Q2 Outlook

Four risks to manage and four opportunities to monetize.

The asset inflected in March and closed the Q1 gap to plan by $52K. The items that could compress flow-through are (a) Property Operations / Insurance / Utilities expense pressure, (b) Cleveland Charge account concentration, (c) a stronger comp set running ahead of Cleveland on March RevPAR, and (d) subdued publishing of guest reviews to external platforms. Peak-season demand signals — including strong trailing-28-day STR performance and healthy April pacing — support both rate discipline and continued extended-stay diversification.

Risk · Variable Expense Pressure

Q1 Property Operations +$20K over budget, Utilities +$7K, Insurance +$7K — combined $34K of cost absorbed before the EBITDA line. Restoring these to plan is the single largest unlock for Q2 flow-through.

Risk · Cleveland Charge Concentration

Cleveland Charge $124K YTD (21.9% of Rooms Revenue) at a $118 ADR — critical block, but heavily rate-dilutive. A schedule change or renegotiation would flow directly through.

Risk · Comp-Set Growth Momentum

Comp set grew March RevPAR +49.6% YoY vs ROOST +31.5% — RGI dropped to 95.5 for the month. A narrowing of the YTD RGI lead (108.7) bears watching as Cleveland hospitality supply begins to modernize.

Risk · External Review Distribution

Publishing submission rate 5.7%; review volume below competitive-set average. Elite sentiment is not fully flowing through to Google/Tripadvisor — a conversion lever left on the table.

Opp · Rate Sustainability Through Shoulder

March's $201 ADR is the first month above $200 since opening. Holding the premium through April–June shoulder dates — particularly on Cavs playoff weekends, Guardians home stands, and event compression — is the highest-value Q2 move.

Opp · Extended-Stay Diversification

Build two to three net-new 30+ night corporate-housing accounts outside Cleveland Charge. National Corporate Housing, Weichert, and Sirva relationships show there is an active corporate-housing market; broadening beyond the Charge ecosystem reduces single-counterparty exposure.

Opp · Rocket/Bedrock Ecosystem Expansion

Rocket Central $10K on 38 RN is a debut. Activating Rocket Mortgage and Rocket Central FOC production (as Detroit has done) is a natural next step with the existing partnership infrastructure.

Opp · Public-Review Conversion

Review Rating #1 in comp set, TA Popularity Index #1. Shifting from 5.7% to 10%+ publishing-submission rate — via widget placement and survey-flow tuning — compounds the sentiment lead into booking conversion.

Strategic summary

The Q1 inflection is real; the Q2 task is to protect it and let peak season do the rest. Rate expansion, extended-stay mix shift, and a lean rooms-operating model are all now in place — the architecture is working. The items that determine whether Q2 converts the Q1 tailwind into a 2026 run-rate step change are: (1) resetting Property Operations / Utilities / Insurance to plan, (2) expanding the corporate-housing book beyond Cleveland Charge, and (3) translating elite Revinate sentiment into more external public reviews. None require a strategic reset — each is operational execution against the existing plan.
